## The Kaefden Line

### Kaefden IV *(the Mage-King)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **First appearance** | KoN — amnesiac on Oceandale beach (player) |
| **Later role** | SoC — King on the Nacastrum throne; commander at Vashirr's Redoubt |
| **Species / faction** | Mage; Kaefden loyalist |
| **Age (approx.)** | ~40 at SoC opening (33 at Nacastrum banishment; crowned at end of KoN) |

**Wound:** Taken from his parents at eight; watched Vashirr murder his father and
abduct his mother; woke on a beach with no memory; rebuilt a kingdom from ashes
while knowing the traitor still breathes.

**Want:** Restore Nacastrum, reunite the mages, and prove the crown was *earned* —
not inherited, not stolen, not a consolation prize from tragedy.

**Voice:** Measured, grief-haunted, never performative. In KoN he is discovering
himself; in SoC he speaks like a man who has rehearsed hard truths in private.
Dry under pressure (*"Vashirr always did love his speeches"*). Does not confuse
mercy with weakness.

**Author notes:** The duology's hidden symmetry — the player *is* the king the
Druid soldier later serves. In fiction, KoN stories stay second-person; SoC throne
and war scenes can shift to third-person limited on Kaefden for contrast, but the
games remain second person throughout.

**Relationships:** Thekia (mentor / council elder); Vashirr (nemesis); parents
(memory-ghosts); SoC protagonist (witness he did not choose but comes to honor).

**Story hooks:** The pendant from his father; the moment memory returns at the
Ring of Malkos; choosing whether to salt Agromanian prisoners; what he says at
Cataracta's memorial stone.

### The First King Kaefden *(historical)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Mythic founder; buried at Varatro Falls with **Kaefden's Blade of Courage** |
| **Appearance** | SoC Act IV — tomb, relic quest |

**Wound:** N/A — ancestral figure.

**Want:** (Posthumous) His blade to serve as the symbol that unites Avasia when
petty factions would stay apart.

**Voice:** Only through elder testimony, tomb inscriptions, and the weight of
tradition. Never speak in his own POV unless writing a deliberate myth-poem.

**Author notes:** The Blade is the title object of game 2. It should feel like
Excalibur without the Arthurian baggage — a *Kaefden* virtue: courage as civic
duty, not personal glory.

### The Banished Younger Brother *(historical — Agroman's founder)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Original schism; never named in source |
| **Appearance** | Prologue story; gate-guard lore in KoN |

**Wound:** Denied the crown; exiled from family and faction.

**Want:** Power, legitimacy, a kingdom that validates his grievance.

**Voice:** Charismatic, grievance-fueled. In historical flashback, let the older
brother and father be sympathetic but firm — the tragedy is that both sides believe
they are saving Avasia.

**Author notes:** Do not conflate with Vashirr. This brother founded Agroman
centuries (or generations) before the games; Vashirr *allied* with that faction.
The faction name is the prince's **middle name: Agroman** ([`../../LORE_ARCHIVE.md`](../../LORE_ARCHIVE.md)).

## The Antagonist

### Vashirr *(Ex-King of Nacastrum)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **First appearance** | KoN — named by Thekia; memory reveal at Ring of Malkos |
| **Physical** | Hooded; scar across one eye; gray staff; Paladin-forging mage |
| **Role** | KoN off-screen villain; SoC on-stage destroyer |

**Wound:** (Speculative but grounded) Opposed by the High Mage's Council when he
believed mages had become a priesthood detached from the ground war. The scar is
the price of acting before consensus — he wears it as proof, not shame.

**Want:** End the Kaefden–Agroman schism by **fusing magic into a single army**
(the **Many Hands doctrine**). Scatter the floating city, disband the council,
forge Paladins, break any crown that restores the old order.

**Voice:** Cold rhetoric, sincere conviction. He speaks like a reformer who has
already paid for his beliefs. Never self-doubts on-page. *"I simply stopped
waiting for councils to agree."*

**Author notes:** Full motivation bible: [`../../VASHIRR.md`](../../VASHIRR.md).
He is Kaefden IV's **former teacher**. Their final duel is philosophical as well
as personal. Do not redeem; do not make him secretly correct.

**Relationships:** Kaefden IV (student turned enemy); Thekia (deposed council
rival); Kimious (assassination as "arithmetic"); Agromanian king (leveraged ally).

**Story hooks:** Many Hands pamphlet in Nacastrum library; memory-restore speech;
Cataracta sermon; throne-room debate; redoubt finale — *"History will know I tried
to end the schism."*

## The Mentor

### Thekia *(High Mage's Council)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **First appearance** | KoN — Old Mage in Oceandale magehouse |
| **Reveal** | KoN endgame — council identity; SoC — Nacastrum library |
| **Physical** | Elderly brunette mage; purple robes in SoC |
| **Age (approx.)** | ~72 at KoN; ~79 at SoC |
| **Lineage** | Malkos-adjacent council blood — Ring-literate, not crown heir |

**Wound:** She helped build the system Vashirr broke — ground houses, sky-heir
compromises, councils that preached restraint while Nacastrum floated above the
war. Watched scatter from Oceandale in a scrying basin; could not reach the tower
in time because she had **chosen the ground**.

**Want:** See the Nacastrum of her childhood restored before she dies; place a
king who **earned** the crown on the throne.

**Voice:** Fluent, sharp, maternal without softness. Delivers lore like someone
who has told this story too many times and still cares. Never performs grief;
**acts** on it. Final KoN line — *"Let us go, my king"* — is the emotional
thesis of game 1.

**Origin (author lock):** Voted for the Council's ground-house compromise and
signed **Oceandale** into the anchor registry for the Restoration heir's family.
Left Nacastrum **voluntarily** soon after Vashirr's coronation — not banished.
Descended to the shore magehouse, bound a **Levitate ward** to wait for the heir
who would wash ashore. Did **not** know Kaefden at the Academy; knew the registry
and Vashirr's star pupil by reputation. Full timeline: [`THEKIA_GROUND_HOUSE.md`](THEKIA_GROUND_HOUSE.md).

**Post-KoN arc:** Restoration architect (library, lean public council) → king's
counterweight (refuses throne seat; tallies Ring costs) → coalition quartermaster
(war camp) → witness rail (anthology couriers, vouches for SoC druid). After the
Age: Chronicler-emeritus at Nacastrum — spell-glass sessions, too old to march.

**Author notes:** She is the saga's constant. KoN: quest-giver. SoC: witness,
escort, quartermaster. Same woman, different war. Blue crystal motif ties her to
Malkos lineage and the Rings. **Thesis:** left the sky on purpose so someone else
could learn to walk the ground.

**Relationships:** Kaefden IV (protégé → king); Vashirr (enemy; debated him when
he was a teacher); SoC protagonist (brief ally); Sylvian elders (professional
respect).

**Story hooks:** Council vote on ground houses · ward binding on descent · basin
night of scatter · ledger handed to Kaefden after crowning · margin note in Many
Hands pamphlet · game 3 spell-glass council.

**Fiction specs:** Stories 2b, 4b, 9b in [`THEKIA_GROUND_HOUSE.md`](THEKIA_GROUND_HOUSE.md).

## KoN Allies & Witnesses

### Dentros *(Druid Scout, Cataracta)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **KoN** | Red fox → cherry-red-haired man; gives lantern; mountain scout |
| **SoC** | Legion recruiter; dies shielding the player from Vashirr |

**Wound:** Caught between druid isolationism and Kaefden alliance.

**Want:** Serve his people; in KoN, help a lost mage; in SoC, train volunteers
who don't yet know what war costs.

**Voice:** Friendly, slightly roguish in KoN; parade-ground warm in SoC (*"Nice of
you to join us!"*). Death is sudden — no extended farewell.

**Author notes:** **Same character both games.** The fox transformation is the
saga's first shapeshift reveal. His SoC death is the emotional inciting incident;
honoring him at the throne is a player choice that Kaefden remembers at the
epilogue.

## SoC Allies & Cataracta

### The SoC Protagonist *(Druid Legionnaire)*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Role** | Player character; named at enlistment |
| **Class** | Wolf (hunter), Bear (guardian), or Fox (scout) |
| **Home** | Southwest Cataracta housing |

**Wound:** Survives genocide; carries Vashirr's message to the king.

**Want:** Serve Cataracta; after the massacre, see the war finished and the dead
honored.

**Voice:** Second person in game; in fiction specs, class flavors combat pledges
and the Vashirr's Redoubt beat. No fixed gender in source.

**Author notes:** Deliberately *not* the Chosen One — a volunteer in an army.

internal monologue humor if desired.
