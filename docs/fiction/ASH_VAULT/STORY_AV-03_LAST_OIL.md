## Prose — *Last Oil*

You are fox-form because the path is narrow and pride is wider than your shoulders.

The Cataracta elder climbs badly for someone who talks beautifully. Her name is not important to this walk; her habit is. She leaves oil at mountain mouths. Not prayer. Not Anula. Plain lamp oil in clay flasks the camp potters still make for a prison that is not there.

The NW mouth is stone where stone should be cliff. Civic seal-marks cracked from inside a century ago and re-chipped by weather. Kaefden surveyors call it *ridge anomaly* on maps that do not deserve you.

You shift to man-shape because the elder prefers talking to faces.

"Why oil," you ask, "if the camp is gone?"

She sits on a boulder that remembers manacles. "Because the dark is not gone."

You sniff. Ash under pine. Pink pulse deep below, so faint you could call it imagination if you had not been trained not to lie to your own nose.

"My grandmother traded ledger copies with an Ofelos clerk," the elder says. "Before the worthy lie. Before the mage trials. When the mountain ate a warden and the crown called it riot."

You flick an ear. Kaefden stories always sound like weather reports until they smell like blood.

"A delver cut the anchor," she says. "Number seven. The ledger used numbers so names would not embarrass the crown when the seal held."

"Did he climb out?"

She looks at the sealed mouth. "Enough climbed for the habit to matter."

She hands you a flask.

"Leave it on the nail. The fox scouts teach it to recruits. Someday a mage will need a lantern and not know why the nail exists."

You shift down, oil in jaws, dignity offended but duty clear.

The nail is iron. The flask is clay. You place it careful as an apology to people whose names were numbers.

Deep below — imagination or heartbeat — something shifts.

You do not wait to find out which.

On the walk back the elder says, "If he asks why the oil, tell him: old habit. The dark isn't."

Two years later you will meet an amnesiac mage on a ridge. You will give him a lantern. You will not say *Delver Seven*. You will not say *Ash Vault*. You will say good luck, my friend, and leap away in fox form because some histories are too heavy for one conversation.

The oil on the nail warms in wind that smells like winter and almost-like-flame.

The mountain keeps its counsel.

You keep the habit.
