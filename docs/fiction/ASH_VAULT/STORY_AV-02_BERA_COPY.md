## Prose — *The Clerk's COPY*

Bera of Ofelos did not believe in united Avasia. Bera believed in copies.

Two witnesses minimum. One for each hand on the wrist, the graffiti in the mountain would say later. Kaefden brought the sentences. Ofelos brought the ink that outlived sermons.

They had attached her to Commander Pell's penal camp because neutrality was supposed to mean *observe, do not obstruct*. In practice it meant sleep in a thin tent, eat soldier porridge, and watch the crown call suffering *policy*.

She had read Malvek's carved sermons on the descent stairs. She had thought: this man worships law the way drowning men worship air.

She had not thought: he will bind pain into stone and the stone will eat him.

---

The tremor came an hour before dusk.

Not collapse — appetite. A long pull through the mountain, as if something inhaled.

Pell's face did not change. That was his talent.

"Seal at dusk," he told Binder Sorin. "Classification: riot suppression. Warden-Magister Malvek, deceased in service."

Bera's pen stopped.

"Deceased in binding," she said, before she could decide to be brave.

Pell looked at her the way file clerks look at inconvenient footnotes. "Deceased in riot," he repeated. "The reliquary experiment is not on any ledger I sign."

"The reliquary is on the mountain," Bera said.

"The mountain is about to be off the maps."

Sorin had already begun to chant. Stonebend was sky anchor work — civic emergency dressed in mage breath. Bera felt the air thicken with oath.

Somewhere below, Delver Seven was still climbing or not climbing. The camp had stopped using names.

---

Smoke rolled out of the mouth first.

Then ash, fine and hot, smelling of charred robes and old iron.

Bera took her oilcloth and her neutral sheets and walked into the smoke because neutrals were not supposed to obstruct and because obstruction was sometimes the only honest posture left.

The niche at Reliquary Approach had been a clerk's nook before it was a shrine to deterrence. Bark stacked dry. Trial records: schism partisans, grove burnings, grain sold to western camps, a Sylvian elder who looked away from a Paladin who did not yet exist under that name.

*Two hands of one body — but who cut the wrist?*

She copied the margin note because it was true.

She copied Malvek's last signed order — *pain binds where oaths fail* — because the crown would call him a martyr of order.

She copied the camp tally: *VII — ring ten — anchor cut* — because numbers were how the penal camp loved its dead.

COPY or LEAVE.

She copied until her hands shook and Sorin's chant hit the final civic seal and the mountain mouth groaned like a door that had wanted to stay open.

---

On the surface Pell did not search her.

Neutrals were politically inconvenient to accuse and politically useful to ignore. Bera walked down the switchback with oilcloth under her arm and ash in her hair and the schism fable living in her throat.

Behind her, folk would begin to say **Ash Vault** the way you say curse words.

Ahead, her registry would file the copies under *mountain incident — disputed* and Ofelos would pretend not to notice when Silvarium elders asked careful questions decades later.

A descendant would stack bark in a pink cavern nook.

A scout would choose COPY or LEAVE again.

A market broker would price memory.

An open ledger would publish what every faction hated equally.

Bera did not know any of that.

She knew only that Restoration summaries would lie because summaries always served the hand that held the knife.

Both hands, she thought. Always both.

She kept walking.
