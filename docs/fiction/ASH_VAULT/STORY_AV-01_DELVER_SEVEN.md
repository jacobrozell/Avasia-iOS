## Prose — *Delver Seven*

You do not get a name at the mouth of the mountain.

Commander Pell gives you oil, a short sword, and a look that has already buried you. The camp is tents and civic seals and men who will not say *penitentiary* aloud. They say *the mine* or *the depth* or nothing at all.

"The reliquary is awake," Pell says. "Delver six did not come back. You are seven."

You check the flask. The oil smells like every lantern you have ever trusted.

"You reach the Heart," he says. "You cut what is growing there. Then you climb. If you cannot climb, we seal at dusk with or without you."

You do not ask what the reliquary is. You have heard the sermons carved in the upper rings. You have heard the crown call it a riot when the turnkeys stopped screaming.

You go down.

---

The iron rings are bolted at wrist height. Kneeling height. Someone scratched a tally beside them — not Kaefden numerals, not Agroman either. A third hand that never learned either alphabet.

Deeper, the pink light begins. Not torchlight. Not magefire. The stone itself pulses, slow as a heartbeat under ice.

You pass a turnkey's whistle on a nail. No whistle on the lip. The shifts ended before you were born and the prison kept working anyway.

*Both. Always both.*

The graffiti is fresh enough to cut your knuckle if you are stupid enough to test it. Two hands on one wrist. You do not know the schism fable by heart — you know the west burns and the crown sentences and the mountain does not care which story you believe.

Your oil ticks down by habit, not by fear yet.

---

Manacle Hall smells of iron that outlived the men it held. Jal's generation, the old miners say when they are drunk. Chain-mine before crown-mine before whatever Malvek called *deterrence*.

In Branching Dark you find a pickaxe and colorless shards — someone loved the pink too much. Spear hole in the back. Discipline, the crown will say later. Monster, the camp will whisper.

You do not take the pickaxe.

Malvek's words start at Forge Descent, cut into slag over a dead forge:

*LET THE SENTENCED SEE THE SENTENCE.*

You read because reading is cheaper than fighting. The Warden-Magister believed in visibility the way priests believe in altars. You wonder, not for the first time, whether the Agroman graffiti and the Kaefden sermons are the same appetite wearing different badges.

Pink Saturation weeps blue into the rock. Anula, the druids would say if any druid stayed this close to a penal mouth. Earth anchor bleeding through sentence. Your flask warms in your pack without sun.

At Reliquary Approach the air tastes like ink and law.

A niche. Bark sheets stacked by someone who was not a prisoner — clerk's hands, neutral registry fold. A choice you do not have time to make properly: copy or leave.

You copy one page. Truth travels. Blame travels faster. You shove the oilcloth into your belt and keep moving because Pell said dusk and the mountain said otherwise long before Pell was born.

---

Ash Choir is where your footsteps double.

Not echo. *Company.*

Names you do not know speak from the fog — condemned, turnkey, a sermon voice that might be Malvek if Malvek still owned a mouth:

*"See what law costs."*

You swing at fog and hit stone. Something wet hits your shoulder. Not blood. Ash, fine as flour, hot as shame.

Malvek's Shade does not apologize when you break it. It says, *"I only wanted them to see,"* and you hate that you understand.

---

Vault Heart is heat without flame.

You understand, then, what the fire room will smell like decades from now when a mage fails a rite that is not yet installed. Ash holding a shape too carefully. Warning with anatomy.

The Sinter is not a dragon. It is reliquary hunger — crystal, manacle, sermon, prisoner, warden, fused until anchor law vomits. It does not roar. It *recognizes* you.

*"You were late,"* it says in a choir of stolen throats. *"You are always late."*

You cut what Pell sent you to cut. The Heart goes cold the way a fever breaks — sudden, guilty, incomplete.

You climb.

---

Surface light blinds you.

Pell does not embrace you. Pell records you.

His scribe writes: *Delver 7 — ring ten breached, anchor cut. Mouth sealed at dusk.*

You ask for your name in the ledger.

Pell looks at you the way the crown looks at mountains it intends to erase.

"The ledger uses numbers," he says. "Go home if you have one."

You do not.

Behind you, Binder Sorin speaks stone closed. The mouth becomes wall. Maps will forget. Folk will curse **Ash Vault** and mothers will tell boys not to mine pink.

You still have one bark page in your oilcloth. You still have oil on your hands.

Years from now a neutral archivist will copy worse sheets in a nook that smells the same. Years from now an amnesiac mage will earn Inflame beside a corpse shaped like warning. Years from now a fox-haired druid will hand a stranger a lantern and say the dark is old habit.

Tonight you are a number that worked.

The ash remembers anyway.
