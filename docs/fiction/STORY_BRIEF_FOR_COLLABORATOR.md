# Avasia — Story Brief

> A shareable summary of where the saga stands: main beats, world rules we've locked in,
> and ideas for the era after the text games. Written for the friend who helped shape the
> original story — not an internal dev bible.

---

## What this is

Avasia is planned as a **long-running saga** across multiple games. Each major era can ship in a different format — the fiction and the engine evolve together.

| Era | Games | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **Age era** | *King of Nacastrum* + *Blade of Courage* | Text adventure (two games, full story) |
| **Commodity Era** | Game 3 (first visual game) | 2D exploration / combat |
| **Future** | TBD | Whatever the world demands |

The meta-promise: *you live through the history of Avasia, and the way you play changes as the world modernizes.*

---

## The one rule everything hangs on

**Power in Avasia must be anchored** — sky, earth, flesh, or civic faith. It cannot float free.

Every spell, relic, and soldier-trick binds magic to a **vessel**. The Age War was fought over *which anchors win*, not over whether magic exists.

| Anchor | Tradition | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Sky** | Mages / towers | Levitate, Inflame, Stonebend; floating Nacastrum; Rings of Malkos |
| **Earth** | Druids / Sylvians | Shapeshifting; **Anula** blue crystals; Great Tree; Stonebend seal |
| **Flesh** | Many Hands | **Paladins** — magic bound into plate and chant |
| **Civic** | First Kaefden | **Blade of Courage** at Varatro Falls; oaths, memorials, coalition law |

---

## The founding sin (where it all starts)

Before living memory, Avasia united under **Kaefden** — until the youngest prince turned a protest in **Aylova** into violence and demanded the crown. Banished with his followers, he founded the **Agromanian** faction. His **middle name, Agroman**, became their banner.

Neither side is purely villain. Both believe the alternative is chaos or tyranny. Every war in the saga is an echo of that room — including who gets to decide **where magic may live**.

---

## The philosophical war (the duology's argument)

| Side | Voice | Claim |
|---|---|---|
| **Restoration** | Kaefden IV | Magic belongs in lineage, towers, and law — *consent and council* |
| **Many Hands** | Vashirr | Magic belongs in every shield — *unity without mage priesthood* |

Vashirr is defeated in game 2. **His argument is not.** The coalition wins the war while inheriting the proof that flesh-anchors work. The next era runs on that uncomfortable compromise.

---

## Locked decisions (won't change casually)

- **Kaefden IV = the King of Nacastrum protagonist.** The amnesiac mage you crown in game 1 is the same king the druid soldier warns seven years later.
- **The SoC protagonist lives.** Game 3 inherits their **myth** (broadsheets, reliquaries, propaganda) — not a resurrection boss or betrayal of the player's save file.
- **We never redeem Vashirr** in the Age-era finale.
- **Age era = two text games only.** No third text adventure. Game 3 is the format leap to 2D.
- **SoC must close the war** and **redistribute how magic is anchored** — not a text cliffhanger.

## Game 1 — *King of Nacastrum*

**Protagonist:** Amnesiac mage (Kaefden heir)  
**Arc:** Recover memory → restore Nacastrum → expose Vashirr → reunite the mages  
**Ending:** Crowned king; mages return through the portal — *"Let us go, my king."*

### Main beats

1. **The Two Brothers** — mythic founding: Aylova schism, Agroman banner, exile.
2. **What the Barbarians Did Not Do** — Nacastrum empties. Vashirr claims a northern horde is coming. Portals swallow citizens. Your father runs toward your mother and dies in blue fire. The horde **never came** — it was a lie.
3. **The Night She Did Not Fly** (Thekia parallel) — Thekia watches from Oceandale in a scrying basin. Grounded by her own law, she holds the ward line while the heir washes ashore.
4. **The King Without Memory** — You wake on a beach beside ruined **Oceandale**. Gate guard lore: Kaefden, Agroman, Ofelos neutrality, anchor law.
5. **The Old Mage's Question** — Thekia tests you, names Vashirr's betrayal and the **Many Hands** rumor, teaches Levitate, sends you to Nacastrum.
6. **The Ground House** — Thekia's backstory: she voted for sky-mages to keep shore oaths, resigned when Vashirr crowned, descended to wait twenty years for an heir she knows by pendant number.
7. **Fox at the Mountain** — Dentros (fox druid) sends you toward the cave. **Cataracta's** Anula gate; Cellious turns you away — you never enter the city you'll one day see burned.
8. **Worthy Blood** — Silvarium: Suformin's Dagger, priestess blessing, librarian's vanity, you cut your palm and learn Stonebend. Worthy blood is sacrifice, not ancestry alone.
9. **Ash in the Pink Cave** — Mountain trial; dead mages in the branches; earn Inflame or join the ash.
10. **The Road Below the City** — Western Road gauntlet; Ring of Malkos; **memory flood** — Academy, Vashirr's coronation, mother's abduction, father's death.
11. **Let Us Go, My King** — Nacastrum restored. Father's corpse in the courtyard; pendant with blue earth-crystal gem. Aylova rally. Mages stream home. Crown acknowledged. War with Agroman only beginning.

**Recurring motifs:** *"Kaefden to Kaefden"* · blue crystal light · Vashirr's eye scar · the pendant.

---

## The seven-year gap

**Oceandale fell** in game 1. **Kaefden IV** has ruled seven years. No Agromanian invasion — until Paladin escalation forces the issue.

Thekia rebuilds the library, refuses a throne seat, runs coalition logistics, and vouches for strangers because she remembers what it cost to be one. Restoration is **administration of grief**, not poetry.

## Game 2 — *Blade of Courage*

**Protagonist:** Named Druid recruit in **Cataracta**  
**Timeline:** Seven years after KoN  
**Arc:** Enlist → fall of Cataracta → warn Kaefden IV → win the coalition war → secure neutral allies via **Kaefden's Blade of Courage**  
**Antagonist:** Vashirr (Agromanian king's right hand; forging Paladins)

### Main beats

1. **Seven Years Without an Army** — Peace in Cataracta. Paladin rumors. **Anula requisition** crates on the Varatho. Fountain coin toss (nothing happens). Bond before burn.
2. **Spirit Animal** — Dentros assigns class: wolf (hunter), bear (guardian), or fox (scout). Kimious is about to speak —
3. **The Day Cataracta Burned** — Vashirr portals an army in. Kimious dies. Dentros dies shielding you. You fight, memorize Vashirr's message for Kaefden IV, witness mass execution of captive druids. You wake in ash.
4. **Through the Ducts of Nacastrum** — Escape via portal chamber; testimony to Thekia in the library.
5. **The Mage-King's Audience** — Kaefden IV has mage ears and seven years of grief. Honor Dentros or report facts. Deliver Vashirr's threat. Blade quest declared: Varatro Falls → Silvarium elders → Ofelos. Coalition marches.
6. **What the Elders Require** — War camp at Aylova. Sylvian elders demand the Blade before they'll speak for Ofelos.
7. **The Blade at Varatro Falls** — First Kaefden's tomb. Paladin Tomb Warden. Blade recovered.
8. **The Neutral City Marches** — Ofelos joins the coalition when the first king's symbol is presented. Neutrality has a price.
9. **Oceandale Ridge** — Northern march. The beach where you woke in game 1 is now a front line. Paladins escalate.
10. **Vashirr's Redoubt** — Dawn on a rocky shore. Kaefden names every crime. Class-specific ward breach. War Mage fight. Vashirr bound in blue crystal; portal collapses. *"This land will forget you too, Kaefden."*
11. **The Age of Courage Closes** — Memorial at Aylova. Thekia: *"We carry them forward."* Kaefden honors you by name. Druid Legion reborn in survivors. Optional Cataracta ruins pilgrimage. **The text era ends.**

### What SoC changes forever (epilogue seeds)

| Shift | What it means for the next era |
|---|---|
| Paladin techniques retained | Licensed **Flesh-Wardens**; illegal **Red Litany** cults |
| Ring network opened | **Ring Roads** — surveyed travel between Malkos nodes |
| Anula commodified | Wartime requisition → peacetime trade; **Anula as currency and magic fuel** |
| Ofelos in the system | Neutrality eroded; new taxes or draft law |
| Messenger myth | Living SoC PC honored; legend weaponized on broadsheets |
| Vashirr bound, not redeemed | Imprisoned; doctrine leaks to cults |

### Institutions (post-war)

- **High Mage's Council** — public spell-glass sessions, no secret cabal
- **Flesh-Warden Corps** — licensed Paladin craft, rotated and rationed
- **Ring Road Survey** — mapped paths between Malkos nodes
- **Crystal Line Company** — Anula relay towers; price and news lines
- **Druid Legion** — SoC survivors; diaspora culture
- **Chronicler ranks** — diegetic historians arguing over the official record

### Cultures (daily texture)

- **Mages** — pointed ears; tower vs shore accent after the scatter
- **Druids** — shapeshift as labor; Cataracta class caste (wolf/bear/fox)
- **Sylvians** — hunt church vs elder law; north migration grief
- **Agromanians** — brotherhood oath meals; middle name as banner
- **Neutrals** — Ofelos coin; resentment when neutrality fails
- **Fishers** — shore meditation; net superstition; Oceandale before the burn

### Economy across eras

```text
Gift era (KoN)        →  Sylvian Anula cartloads; fountain coins
Requisition (SoC)     →  Crates, clippings, Kimious's warning
Commodity (game 3)    →  Graded shards, rent, ward-fuel, line news
```

**Anula** is blue earth-anchor crystal — on Cataracta's gates, mage pendants, shrine gifts. In the Age era it was **gift and ritual**. After the war it becomes **money and magic fuel** — scarce, graded, stamped. Every spell is a budget choice.

## The next era — Commodity Era (Game 3)

**Working name:** Commodity Era — the age after courage, when earth-anchor power is weighed, priced, and spent.

**Format:** First **2D** game. Towns, mines, and relay towers are visible geography — not rooms described one line at a time. The parser Age ends when the continent becomes **legible**: charted, priced, networked.

### The trilogy question

```text
ERA 1 (KoN)  — Where is magic ALLOWED to live?     (Towers. Heirs. Councils.)
ERA 2 (SoC)  — Who can be FORCED to carry it?      (Paladins. Legions. War.)
ERA 3 (2D)   — Who can AFFORD to spend it?         (Mines. Markets. Debt. Myth.)
```

Vashirr wanted magic in every hand. The Commodity Era delivers magic in every **wallet** — still unequal, now on a ledger.

### The three-blade horror stack

```text
Anula economy    →  "You can't afford to be safe."
Flesh garrisons  →  "We keep you safe but never say what it costs us."
Red Litany cults →  "Safety is a lie — bleed and be free."
```

**Flesh magic cannot be fully banned** after the war — borders still need garrisons, earth-magic costs crystal the poor can't afford. So the crown licenses **Flesh-Wardens** (rotated, rationed). Partial bans breed black markets. Underground **Red Litany** cells worship the **echo** left in scar tissue — Many Hands without order, not Vashirr's authorized program. Each binding erodes the mind.

### Opening town: Splitpath Station

Built on the same crossing from KoN's **Splitpath** — now a surveyed rail-head where Ring Road freight meets crystal-line relays.

**Player fantasy:** Dock clerk or mine courier in a company town — not the Messenger, but living under their face on a broadsheet.

**First ten minutes teach:**
1. Anula is **money** (rent due in graded shards)
2. Anula is **magic** (spend it for wards and relays)
3. Flesh **echoes** exist (garrison chant; Red Litany whispers in the pits)

**Opening beats:**
1. Wake — shift bell; landlord; rent = 12 graded shards in three days
2. Market board — Anula prices; Sylvian elder protest on the line news
3. Job offer — courier packet; pays 8 shards (not enough alone)
4. Choice — overtime mine run vs pawn heirloom vs skip the ward
5. Chant riot at garrison; pit rumors
6. Broadsheet: living Messenger denies sainthood; PC recognizes the name from family lore / Cataracta diaspora

### Who controls Anula (faction hooks)

| Actor | Posture |
|---|---|
| **Silvarium elders** | Sacred enclosure; oppose open-pit mining near the Great Tree |
| **Kaefden crown** | Charter law, reserve vault, garrison requisitions |
| **Ofelos merchants** | Neutral no longer — finance lines and debt instruments |
| **Agroman remnants** | Western pits reopen under treaty labor |
| **Cataracta diaspora** | Miners, smugglers, druids who remember when crystal was tossed in fountains |
| **Cartels / dust-runners** | Black market when crown cracks down |

Game 3 threats grow from **what the war built** — not resurrecting the SoC protagonist as a villain:
- Paladin reliquary or forged "saint"
- Corrupted ring node or Anula monopoly
- Institution mining Sylvian earth under Kaefden law
- Red Litany cell in a company town

The horror is **the system**, not a betrayal of the player's save file.

### Social commentary (intentional, embedded in play)

Parallels to extractive capitalism emerge from systems and NPC arguments — not narrator sermons. No faction is a clean mouthpiece. A Sylvian elder opposes mines but benefited from wartime requisition. A Kaefden reformer wants public wards funded by tax. A mine boss employs Cataracta refugees.
