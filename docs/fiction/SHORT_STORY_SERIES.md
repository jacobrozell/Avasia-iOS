# Avasia — Short Story Series Spec

> The full Age-era saga as an **author's anthology**: discrete stories that could
> stand alone in a magazine or collection while building toward the war's end.
> Numbered in **in-world chronological order**.

---

## Series overview

| Volume | Span | Stories | Protagonist lens |
|---|---|---|---|
| **I — The Floating Crown** | Schism → KoN ending | 1–8 | Amnesiac mage → Kaefden IV crowned |
| **II — Blade of Courage** | SoC opening → Age epilogue | 9–16 | Druid legionnaire |
| **III — TBD** | Post–Age era (game 3, 2D) | — | New generation |

**Series thesis:** Avasia breaks apart when power fears sharing the table — and
mends when scattered peoples choose loyalty over bloodline. Magic, shapeshifting,
and blue crystal light mark the worthy.

**Recurring refrain:** *"Kaefden to Kaefden"* · *"Let us go, my king"* · the scar
on Vashirr's eye · the pendant's blue gem.

### Story 1 — *The Two Brothers of Aylova*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN — gate-guard lore (`STORY.md` §2.1) |
| **POV** | Third person past (historical myth) |
| **Length target** | 3,000–4,000 words |
| **Cast** | The king; elder prince; younger prince; court witnesses |

**Synopsis:** Before living memory, Avasia united under Kaefden — until the
youngest son turned a protest in Aylova into violence and demanded the crown.
Banished with his followers, he founds the Agromanian faction. The elder line
endures. Neither side is purely villain; both believe chaos or tyranny is the
alternative.

**Scene beats:**
1. Peaceful Aylova — the youngest speaks in the square.
2. Violence — the king's guard intervenes; blood on cobblestones.
3. Private audience — the younger spites his father; the elder brother stands with the king.
4. Banishment decree — exile beyond Kaefden borders.
5. Coda — Agroman rises; hatred outlives the brothers. The faction name is the
   prince's **middle name**.

**Theme:** Foundational sin. Every war in the saga is an echo of this room.

**Tone:** Earnest myth. No modern jokes — this is the one story told as legend.

**Connections:** Story 8 (Kimious cites Oceandale's shadow); Story 12 (Kaefden
vs Vashirr as personal schism replay).

### Story 2 — *What the Barbarians Did Not Do*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN — Thekia's doubt (`STORY.md` §2.3) |
| **POV** | Third limited — a Nacastrum citizen during banishment |
| **Length target** | 4,000–5,000 words |
| **Cast** | Vashirr; crowd of mages; Kaefden IV (unnamed, age 33); player's parents |

**Synopsis:** On the night Nacastrum empties, Vashirr claims barbarians will
destroy the floating city. Portals swallow citizens by the hundred. One teacher
watches a man run toward his wife and die in blue fire. The king's true allegiance
is visible only to those who ask why a sky-city feared a northern horde that never
came.

**Scene beats:**
1. Alarm — blinding light in the courtyard.
2. Vashirr beside an Agromanian envoy — portals open.
3. Abductions — women and children dragged through.
4. The father runs — Vashirr kills him.
5. Scatter — last image before darkness: falling, falling.
6. Epilogue — Oceandale burns "last week"; the teacher washes ashore with no name.

**Theme:** Lies that save tyrants. The mystery Thekia names in the magehouse.

**Tone:** Tragic, tight. No humor.

**Connections:** Story 6 (memory restoration); Story 7 (father's corpse); entire
SoC (Vashirr's ongoing alliance).

### Story 3 — *The King Without Memory*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN Act I — beach, gate, Oceandale |
| **POV** | Second person present (game voice) |
| **Length target** | 5,000–6,000 words |
| **Cast** | Player; old gate guard; merchant; ruins of Oceandale |

**Synopsis:** You wake on a beach beside a dying port city. An old guard at the
broken gate tells you how Avasia split, how Agromanians sacked the coast, and how
the mages vanished from their floating city. You know nothing — not your name, not
why ships fish peacefully on the horizon while corpses stack in the graveyard.

**Scene beats:**
1. Waves — stretch, disorientation, blue robe, pointed ears.
2. Gate lore — Kaefden, Agroman, Neutrals of Ofelos.
3. Ruined trading post — merchant's wife taken.
4. Graveyard — sword from a dead soldier.
5. West — the magehouse door waits.

**Theme:** Identity as absence. War's cost before the quest begins.

**Tone:** Earnest opening + first meta beats (yoga on the beach, absurd death
warnings optional in prose adaptation).

**Connections:** Story 4 (Thekia); Story 8 (Oceandale as recurring wound).

### Story 4 — *The Old Mage's Question*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN — magehouse meeting |
| **POV** | Second person present |
| **Length target** | 4,000 words |
| **Cast** | The Old Mage (Thekia); player |

**Synopsis:** The magehouse door slams shut. An elderly mage tests whether you
remember your faction — *"What faction do the mages represent?"* — and names
Vashirr's betrayal. She gives you Levitate and a quest: unlock Nacastrum, unite
the people. You leave knowing someone believes you are terribly lost, and terribly
important.

**Scene beats:**
1. Door seals — unseen magic.
2. Recognition — *"Mage to mage; Kaefden to Kaefden."*
3. Vashirr exposition — barbarian lie, Agromanian alliance.
4. Spell — Levitate taught.
5. Quest declared — gates of Nacastrum.

**Theme:** Mentorship as inheritance. The council's last agent finds her king.

**Tone:** Earnest, intimate. Thekia's sharpest dialogue.

**Connections:** Story 11 (Thekia in library); Story 12 (same woman, seven years later).

### Story 5 — *Fox at the Mountain*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN — Dentros, mountain, druid gate |
| **POV** | Second person present |
| **Length target** | 4,500 words |
| **Cast** | Dentros; Cellious; druid hunters; black wolf |

**Synopsis:** A red fox becomes a man with cherry-red hair and gives you a lantern.
He sends you toward the cave where Old Mages hid their secrets. At Cataracta's
stone gate, hunters shapeshift — man to wolf — until you prove you are a mage.
Chief Cellious turns you away: their king fears spies. You never enter the city
you will one day see burned.

**Scene beats:**
1. Fox encounter — transformation reveal.
2. Lantern gift — cave foreshadowed.
3. Blue crystal gate — six hunters.
4. Wolf scare — proof of magehood (ears, Levitate, Stonebend if earned early).
5. Cellious apology — class structure explained; entry denied.

**Theme:** Allies behind closed borders. Shapeshifting as trust problem.

**Tone:** Wonder + tension. First druid warmth.

**Connections:** Story 9 (Dentros recruiter); Story 10 (Cataracta destroyed);
Character bible — Dentros spans both games.

### Story 6 — *Worthy Blood*

**Theme:** Sacrifice vs. entitlement. Sylvian–mage pact under strain.

**Tone:** Earnest with character comedy (librarian's wolf threat, priestess's *"42"*).

**Connections:** Story 13 (Silvarium elders in SoC); Story 14 (Blade as second
worthy-blood test for Ofelos).

### Story 7 — *Ash in the Pink Cave*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN — mountain cave, Inflame puzzle |
| **POV** | Second person present |
| **Length target** | 5,000 words |
| **Cast** | Dead mage in fireball room; corpse trails in branches |

**Synopsis:** Lantern light on pink crystals. Branching passages littered with
miners, bitten corpses, hanging cages. A burnt mage slumps in the corner of the
fire room — someone else sought Inflame and failed. You solve the four-symbol rite
or join the ash. The cave teaches that the quest kills indiscriminately.

**Scene beats:**
1. Dark entrance — swim, not fly (stalactite death if wrong).
2. Main cavern — branch exploration, symbol clues.
3. Fire room — dead mage foreshadow.
4. Puzzle — three failures burn you alive.
5. Inflame earned — fire in your hands.

**Theme:** Competitors on the same road. Magic has a body count.

**Tone:** Horror-adventure; meta humor on wrong commands optional.

**Connections:** Story 15 (Paladin tomb warden — same dungeon energy in SoC).

### Story 8 — *The Road Below the City*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN Act III–IV — Western Road gauntlet, Ring of Malkos |
| **POV** | Second person present |
| **Length target** | 6,000–7,000 words |
| **Cast** | Agromanian ambush; Thekia (escort); Ring of Malkos |

**Synopsis:** Inflame breaks an ambush; Stonebend parts stone spires; the memory
bridge repeats until you jump into water that is not water — and Nacastrum hangs
above you like a second moon. Thekia activates the Ring with the Staff. You step
through and remember everything: the Academy, Vashirr's coronation when you were
thirteen, your mother's abduction, your father's death. You scream. She waits.

**Scene beats:**
1. Agromanian ambush — Inflame or die.
2. Stone spires — Stonebend.
3. Memory bridge — surreal repetition; jump.
4. Platform — floating city revealed.
5. Fetch Thekia — Staff, escort, activation.
6. **Memory flood** — full reveal (`STORY.md` §7).
7. Thekia names herself — High Mage's Council.

**Theme:** Recovery of self is worse than amnesia. The quest was always personal.

**Tone:** Peak earnest tragedy. Minimal humor.

**Connections:** Story 2 (same night as banishment); Story 12 (Kaefden remembers
Vashirr's courtyard).

### Story 9 — *Let Us Go, My King*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN Act V — Nacastrum, Aylova rally, ending |
| **POV** | Second person present |
| **Length target** | 5,000 words |
| **Cast** | Thekia; father's corpse; exiled mages; Aylova crowd |

**Synopsis:** Nacastrum's silver streets are ransacked but restorable. In the
courtyard you find your father's body and take his pendant — silver, blue gem.
Thekia opens a Ring to Aylova and rallies the banished. Mages and non-mages
stream home. The city repopulates. She looks at you and says: *"Let us go, my
king."* The work has just started.

**Scene beats:**
1. Ruined floating city — towers, silence.
2. Father's corpse — vow, pendant.
3. Aylova speech — Vashirr's alliance denounced.
4. Return through portal — homecoming.
5. Thekia's final lines — crown acknowledged.
6. Coda — war with Agroman not over, only beginning.

**Theme:** Restoration is not victory. Crowning is responsibility.

**Tone:** Bittersweet triumph. Volume I closes.

**Connections:** Story 10 (seven-year gap); Story 12 (same king on throne).

**Theme:** The calm is borrowed time. Bond before burn.

**Tone:** Warm Act I + light meta. Highest humor density in Volume II.

**Connections:** Story 3 (Oceandale parallel); Story 5 (Dentros returns); Story 16

## Author checklist before drafting prose

- [ ] Second person for game-aligned stories; third only for myth (Story 1) and optional Kaefden interludes
- [ ] Preserve verbatim game dialogue where quoted in bibles
- [ ] Balance earnest tragedy with meta humor per KoN register — heavier in Act I SoC, absent in massacre and memory scenes
- [ ] Blue crystal / pendant / staff motif in every volume
- [ ] *"Kaefden to Kaefden"* at least once per volume
- [ ] Do not redeem Vashirr
- [ ] Kaefden IV and KoN protagonist are the same person — never contradict
- [ ] Age era ends at Story 20; game 3 is a new format and generation
