## III-M01 — *Splitpath Station, First Shift*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | `GAME3_OPENING.md` · KoN Splitpath LOOK |
| **POV** | Second person present — dock clerk **(default game 3 PC)** |
| **Length target** | 5,000–6,000 words (Act I spine) |
| **When** | Commodity Era opening |
| **Cast** | Landlord; relay clerk; Warden sergeant; dust-runner kid |

### Synopsis

You wake to shift bell and rent due — **12 graded shards** in three days. Walk the
market board: Anula up after Sylvian protest. Courier job pays eight. Messenger
face on broadsheet is not the real person. Toss shard in fountain — nothing
happens. Chant riot at garrison; **Red Litany** whispered in the pit.

### Scene beats

1. **Wake** — landlord; company scrip trap.
2. **Market board** — prices, line news, Kaefden on coin.
3. **Job** — relay packet; overtime mine fork.
4. **Fountain** — joke + tragedy; diaspora luck ritual.
5. **Riot** — Wardens break chant; pit rumor.
6. **Hook** — recognize Messenger name from family lore / Cataracta diaspora.

### Theme

Anula = money + magic + survival.

### Tone

Systemic unfairness playable; no sermon.

### Connections

III-M02–07 · W-08 Splitpath industrialized.

### Key dialogue

- Landlord: *"Rent is anchor law for the poor. Pay or drift."*

### Synopsis

Dentros — *"the plate saves me"* — without knowing the source. Licensed flesh

## III-M04 — *Red Litany in the Pit*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | `SAGA.md` §8.11 Red Litany |
| **POV** | Third limited — miner **Dell** |
| **Length target** | 5,000 words |
| **When** | Mine district north of Splitpath |
| **Cast** | Dell; cult cell; company foreman |

### Synopsis

Company scrip isn't enough for ward-fuel at home. **Red Litany** offers binding
without license — bleed and be free, they say. Dell joins; mine **collapse** follows.
Survivors blame cult; company blames "unregulated stacking." System wins either way.

### Scene beats

1. **Shift** — dust, quotas, rent letter.
2. **Recruitment** — pit chapel; Many Hands twisted.
3. **Rite** — flesh binding without rotation; ecstatic horror.
4. **Collapse** — echo stacks; minds fray.
5. **After** — foreman raises prices; Wardens seal pit.
6. **Coda** — broadsheet: *"Paladin deserters"* — wrong name, right fear.

### Theme

Third blade of war debt — cult as counterfeit Many Hands.

### Tone

Horror earnest; no redeeming cult.

### Connections

B-05 · III-M03 · `SAGA.md` three-blade stack.

## III-M05 — *The Messenger They Invented*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | Survivor's legend · `SAGA.md` §8.7 |
| **POV** | Third limited — broadsheet editor **Quinn** |
| **Length target** | 4,000 words |
| **When** | Commodity Era — capital or Splitpath |
| **Cast** | Quinn; real SoC survivor (cameo); reliquary merchant |

### Synopsis

The real **Messenger** — SoC protagonist, alive — denies sainthood in an interview.
Quinn prints the denial **small** and the legend headline **large** because sales
fund the crystal line. Survivor's myth weaponized; living person walks out angry.

### Scene beats

1. **Interview** — Messenger tired, not holy.
2. **Headline meeting** — revenue vs truth.
3. **Layout** — denial in footnote.
4. **Street** — reliquary seller profits.
5. **Messenger confronts** — Quinn has no good answer.
6. **Coda** — second printing unchanged.

### Theme

Survivor's legend — system eats heroes.

### Tone

Media satire, saga-tragic.

### Connections

Story 20 · III-M01 broadsheet · **Rule:** PC not villain.

### Key dialogue

- Messenger: *"I didn't survive to become your currency."*

3. **Diaspora case** — names matter.

5. **Kaefden** — one nod; no speech.
6. **Coda** — B-02 red string on wall visible in description.

### Theme

Truth as administered resource.

### Tone

Institutional, cold hope.

### Connections
