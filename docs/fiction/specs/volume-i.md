## I-M01 — *Paladin Zero*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | KoN Western Road ambush · `VASHIRR.md` · proto-Paladin chant-plate |
| **POV** | Third limited — **Corren**, Agromanian foot soldier |
| **Length target** | 4,000–5,000 words |
| **When** | Months before KoN — Vashirr's early workshop |
| **Cast** | Corren; Vashirr; workshop surgeon-mage; sergeant |

### Synopsis

Corren has never been clever — only loyal. Vashirr offers the Many Hands rite:
magic pressed into plate until the chant becomes a second heartbeat. The binding
hurts worse than any wound. When it holds, Corren can turn a blade with a word
he did not choose. He weeps and thanks the king who finally made him *useful*.
The sergeant posts him on the western road to test whether mages bleed the same.

### Scene beats

1. **Muster** — Agromanian camp; Many Hands pamphlet read aloud; Corren volunteers.
2. **Workshop** — Vashirr explains consent that isn't consent: *"You asked."*
3. **Binding** — chant, plate, echo in the skull; Corren screams, then stands.
4. **First drill** — blocks Inflame; doesn't understand how; likes that he doesn't.
5. **Road post** — watches for blue robes; hums the chant while waiting.
6. **Coda** — KoN ambush echo: he will meet a mage with fire in his hands.

### Theme

Many Hands **sincerity** — the horror is that it works for true believers.

### Tone

Earnest tragedy. No cartoon evil — Corren is sympathetic.

### Connections

I-M08 (*Net in the Forest*) · II-M08 (*Paladin Seventeen*) · KoN ambush room.

### Key dialogue

- *"I never had a gift. You gave me one. I won't waste it."*
- Vashirr: *"Usefulness is the only mercy the schism left us."*

## I-M02 — *The Academy Election*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | `CHARACTERS.md` Vashirr/Thekia · Kaefden as student |
| **POV** | Third limited — **Lira**, third-year academy mage |
| **Length target** | 5,000 words |
| **When** | ~15 years before KoN |
| **Cast** | Vashirr; Thekia; Lira; unnamed star pupil (Kaefden IV, age ~18) |

### Synopsis

The academy elects a **court teacher**. Vashirr campaigns on Many Hands:
magic belongs in every hand that holds a shield. Thekia argues restraint from
the council gallery. Lira watches the star pupil — quiet, gifted, unsettlingly
fair — ask whether a soldier who never chose the gift is still a citizen. The
room goes still. Vashirr smiles like he has been waiting for the question.

### Scene beats

1. **Nomination** — faculty split; pamphlets in the halls.
2. **Thekia's speech** — *"Handles are not hands."*
3. **Vashirr's reply** — Oceandale fishing colony cited; towers did not save the coast.
4. **Star pupil's question** — consent vs conscription; Kaefden unnamed.
5. **Vote** — Vashirr wins faculty plurality; council retains veto (for now).
6. **Corridor** — Thekia to Lira: *"He believes it. That is why we will lose."*

### Theme

Duology argument born in a classroom.

### Tone

Political, intimate. No combat.

### Connections

`THEKIA_GROUND_HOUSE.md` Story 4b · Story 2 · II-M01.

### Key dialogue

- Star pupil: *"If we put magic in a man who cannot refuse it, are we Kaefden or Agroman?"*
- Thekia: *"The question is correct. The election was already wrong."*

## I-M03 — *Malkos's First Ring*

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Canon anchor** | Rings of Malkos · `STORY.md` §2.4 · anchor law myth |
| **POV** | Third person past (legend) |
| **Length target** | 3,000–4,000 words |
| **Cast** | Malkos; court; first sky-city engineers |

### Synopsis

Before Nacastrum floated, **Malkos** levitated the royal tower and could not
come down. Power without place becomes war — the court panics. Malkos names the
courtyard stone; the spell **anchors**; he walks. He carves the first Ring where
his feet landed and teaches: *"Power must be tied to a place, or it drifts."*

### Scene beats

1. **Ascent** — tower rises; celebration turns to fear when he cannot descend.
2. **Court debate** — kill the king-mage or learn.
3. **Naming** — Malkos speaks the courtyard; Levitate releases.
4. **First Ring** — silver groove, blue gem set; teleport to Aylova gate.
5. **Coda** — floating city approved **only** with ground Rings at every node.

### Theme

Anchor law as discovered physics, not theology.

### Tone

Earnest myth — same register as Story 1 (*Two Brothers*).

### Connections

Story 1 · Magehouse staff speech · all Ring scenes.

### Key dialogue

- *"I would rather be king of one stone than god of every sky."*

## I-M04 — *The Merchant's Wife*

| **POV** | Third limited — **Sera**, merchant's wife; then structural silence |
| **Length target** | 2,500–3,500 words |
| **When** | Oceandale sack week (parallel scatter) |
| **Cast** | Sera; husband (off-page); Agromanian portal team |

### Synopsis

Sera is not mage-blood — she sold dried cod and kept the books. Agromanian
portals open in the lane; she is taken because her husband **resisted** and
because ground-oathed households are on a list she never saw. Inside the threshold:
light, hands, a chant that is not Many Hands yet but will rhyme with it. No rescue.
No name spoken in the prose after she crosses. The husband's store burns in the
story's last line — KoN trading post grief without closure.

### Scene beats

1. **Ordinary morning** — ledger, nets, rumor of sky falling.
2. **Lane** — portals; husband shouts; she is pulled.
3. **Threshold** — disorientation; other women; no geography.
4. **Silence** — section break; POV withheld.
5. **Coda** — husband at ruins; player will meet him later with no answers.

### Theme

Scatter cost on **unnamed** civilians; horror without resolution.

### Tone

Tragic, tight. No humor. **Do not** reveal her fate — game 3 territory.

### Connections

I-M04 · Story 3 trading post · B-02 Mother's Thread.

### Key dialogue

- Husband (last heard): *"She didn't sign your registry!"*

5. **Coda** — Edda brings ledger to gate guard; KoN graveyard church flavor.

### Theme

Civic anchor vs tower — Oceandale deserved to stand.

### Tone

Earnest, angry, quiet.

### Connections
